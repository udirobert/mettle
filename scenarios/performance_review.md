# Performance Review: Senior Employee Termination

## Stakes

Terminating a senior engineering manager who has been with the company for 8
years. The conversation must be clear, respectful, and legally defensible.

## Counterpart

Marcus Chen, Senior Engineering Manager. He joined early, built critical systems,
and has deep relationships across the organization. His performance has declined
over 18 months despite coaching. He is emotional, loyal to the company, and will
likely feel betrayed.

## Known objections

- "I built this team. You can't do this to me."
- "The goals changed after the reorg. I wasn't set up to succeed."
- "What about my equity? My severance? My references?"
- "This will destroy the team's morale."

## User's objective

Deliver the decision clearly and compassionately. Avoid ambiguity that invites
debate. Ensure Marcus understands the decision is final, the support available,
and the next steps. Protect the company from legal exposure while treating him
with dignity.

## Opening transcript seed

**Marcus:** "I got the calendar invite. What's this about? Is everything okay?"
